The mod's core theme is magic and alchemy.

I need to target the needs of a Minecraft playthrough — modded or not — and give the player a reason to use a magic system beyond mere style.

That reason is easy to find, and it makes the mod not mandatory but a "QOL" (quality-of-life) layer.

Introducing the idea:

Magic is everywhere. In plants, animals, monsters, blocks, fluids, ores, dimensions... everywhere. That's where alchemy and magic come in.

Alchemy: Transmute / Amplify.

Magic: Create / Transform.

Alchemy doesn't reinvent the wheel, it reinforces it. An alchemist crafts all sorts of tools and brews to improve what they already own — change a material, strengthen it, grant it certain properties. Alchemy is the art of reshaping things.

Magic makes concrete what isn't. Magic doesn't follow our world's rules and therefore doesn't interact with it. With a spell you create new things; the only limits are imagination and the required power.

Let's find a balance. These two opposing forces must serve one another. Let's look at their impact on the world:

Alchemy isn't free — it's an equivalent exchange between our world and the Alternate World. Everything brought back from the Alternate World is replaced by something from ours. An alchemist must keep the balance at every moment, at the risk of causing a collision between the two realities and triggering irreversible effects on themselves or the world.

Magic is powerful, but it's a bottomless pit that draws in all your strength. It starts with your [[Vital Energy]], then your hunger, and ends by killing you once you've been drained.

But what would a mage who amplifies their own powers with alchemy achieve? Or an alchemist who sates the Alternate World with their own magical creations?

That's the whole goal of the Alchiromancers: they seek the perfect balance that lets them draw endlessly from both forces.
