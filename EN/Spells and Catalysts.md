## 1. The anatomy of a Spell in Alchiromancy

Every spell is structured in 4 distinct phases:

- **The Oneiric State (No Catalyst):** the spell is intangible, utilitarian and passive (here, a fixed light source that doesn't burn).

- **The "[[Vital Energy]]" cost:** a loss of Vital Energy points.

- **The Casting Matrix:** a precise combination of items in the right hand and left hand, with a "channeling" animation (the items drawing together, trembling).

- **The Translation (With Catalyst):** using a catalyst that gives the spell physical consistency and turns it into something else.

In Alchiromancy, spells in themselves have very little use. Without external intervention they can at best help you see certain things, light up an area, or alter your perception of reality.

For a spell to gain "consistency" it must receive a catalyst; without one it's useless and eventually fades away.

Each catalyst has its own purpose, and not all spells and catalysts mix — combining the wrong ones can create unknown and dangerous effects.
