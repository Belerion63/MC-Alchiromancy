Magic is powerful, but because it's superimposed onto our reality you can only rarely interact with it. Magical fire doesn't burn, oneiric water doesn't wet, telluric force won't trigger any earthquake, and even an impressive aerial storm won't stir a single leaf.

So what's the point?

That's where catalysts come in. Catalysts are stones that translate magical logic into our world's logic. Focusing a spell into our reality sheds the boundless scope of its wild, oneiric form — that's the "weakening" — but it's the only way to make magic actually *do* something.

That doesn't make it any less effective. Powerful mages have raised palaces of ice, others have burned whole countries, some have dug tunnels in a few days, and still others have cleared the sky with a single breath. But all of it comes at a price. Magic draws on your deepest reserves — sometimes to the point of killing you.

Conjuring a tiny flamelet? Almost nothing — that's not what will exhaust you.
Conjuring a mighty blaze able to annihilate a whole dungeon? Impossible — you'd be dead before you even finished the incantation.

Just digging a 500 m tunnel takes dozens of seasoned mages.
