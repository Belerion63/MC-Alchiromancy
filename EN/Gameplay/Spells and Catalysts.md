## 1. The Anatomy of a Spell in Alchiromancy

Every spell is structured into 4 distinct phases:

- **The Oneiric State (No Catalyst):** the spell is intangible, utilitarian, and passive (here, a fixed light source that doesn't burn).

- **The cost in "[Vital Energy](Vital%20Energy.md)":** loss of Vital Energy points.

- **The Casting Matrix:** a precise combination of items in the right hand and left hand, with a "channeling" animation (bringing the items together, trembling).

- **The Translation (With Catalyst):** the use of a catalyst that gives the spell physical consistency and turns it into something else.

In Alchiromancy, spells by themselves have very little use. Without outside intervention, at best they can help you see certain things, light up an area, or alter your perception of reality.

For a spell to gain "consistency," it must receive a catalyst — without that, it's useless and eventually fades away.

Each catalyst has its own purpose, and not all spells and catalysts mix — that can create unknown and dangerous effects.
