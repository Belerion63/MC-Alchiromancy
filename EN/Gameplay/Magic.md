Magic is powerful, but since it lies over our reality, we can unfortunately only very rarely interact with it. Magical fire doesn't burn, oneiric water doesn't wet, telluric power won't cause any earthquake, and even an impressive aerial storm won't move a single tree leaf.

So what's the point?

That's where catalysts come in. Catalysts are stones that let you translate magical logic into the logic of our world. But the process is costly and can quickly weaken the mage. It also alters the spell, giving effects different from those of the original spell.

That doesn't make it any less effective — powerful mages have raised palaces of ice, others have burned entire countries, some have dug tunnels in a matter of days, and others still have cleared the sky with a single breath. But all of that comes at a price. Magic draws from your deepest reserves, to the point of sometimes killing you.

Summoning a small flamelet? Easy! That's not what's going to exhaust you.

Summoning a mighty blaze capable of annihilating an entire dungeon? Impossible — you'd be dead before you even finished the incantation.

Just digging a 500m tunnel requires dozens of experienced mages.
