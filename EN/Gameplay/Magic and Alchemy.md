Magic doesn't interact with our world. It lies over it like a thin layer of dew. Well, that is, unless you bring alchemy into it.

Magic and alchemy are two different forces, not drawing from the same reserves. They oppose each other by nature, which makes them powerful allies once tamed.

A sufficiently gifted mage may be able to temporarily alter reality — their magic would be so powerful that its overlay would become palpable.

Let's take an example:

A basic fire-creation spell. Magical fire doesn't burn and doesn't consume; it's like a holographic lighter.

Now imagine an alchemist uses that ability to siphon this fire and transmute it with the Alternate World — what would happen? This fire isn't from our world, so consequently it has no equivalent in the Alternate World.

Well, the effect would be to create a brand new reality within the Alternate World. The world is logical, and when something falls outside its logic, it corrects itself and creates an equivalent element. The alchemist thus makes concrete what magic alone could not do — alchemical fire burns, and far more intensely than normal fire, and allows the creation of alloys directly through the portal.

That's the whole principle of alchiromancy! Drawing on both alchemy and magic at once!
